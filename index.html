Tax Benefits on Insurance in India

Insurance isn't only about protection — it's one of India's smartest legal tax-saving tools under the Income Tax Act. Here's how much you can save every financial year.

Section 80C

Life Insurance Premiums

Claim deduction up to ₹1,50,000 per year on life insurance premiums paid for yourself, spouse and children. This includes term plans, ULIPs and endowment policies.

Up to ₹1.5 Lakh / year
Section 80D

Health Insurance Premiums

Deduct up to ₹25,000 for self, spouse and kids — and an additional ₹50,000 for senior-citizen parents. Preventive check-ups up to ₹5,000 are also included.

Up to ₹75,000 / year
Section 10(10D)

Maturity & Death Benefits

Payouts from life insurance policies — maturity, bonus and death benefits — are fully tax-exempt, subject to conditions specified by the Income Tax Act of India.

100% Tax-Free Payouts

*Tax benefits are subject to prevailing income-tax laws and conditions. Please consult a qualified tax advisor for your specific situation.

FAQs

Questions? We've Got Answers

Everything you wanted to know about working with SAS Policy Value Hub.

Do you charge anything for consultation?

No, absolutely not. Our consultation, plan comparison, document filling and policy issuance assistance are 100% free for you. As a licensed advisor, we're compensated by the insurer only after you choose and pay for a policy — this payment comes from the insurer's standard distribution budget, not added to your premium. That's why you'll never see any hidden or "consultation" charge on your quote.

Which insurance companies do you work with?

We're empanelled with 50+ leading IRDAI-registered insurers — including HDFC Ergo, Star Health, ICICI Lombard, Manipal Cigna, Care Health, Niva Bupa, HDFC Life, TATA AIG, Bajaj Allianz, SBI General and many more. This lets us run an unbiased side-by-side comparison and shortlist the best option for your specific profile, budget and health history.

Can you help with claims for a policy bought elsewhere?

Yes, we can. Reach out with your policy document and incident details — we'll guide you through the claim process, help you prepare paperwork, and coordinate with the insurer on your behalf wherever permissible. While formal claim processing remains between you and your insurer, our advisory support often makes a big difference in speeding up settlements.

How long does it take to issue a new policy?

It depends on the insurance type. Motor insurance can be issued in under 10 minutes — entirely online. Basic health policies typically take 2–6 hours after KYC; more complex cases with medical tests may take 24–72 hours. Term life policies usually take 3–7 days as underwriting checks medical history. We keep you informed at every step.

Are my personal details secure with SAS?

Absolutely. We follow strict data-privacy protocols aligned with India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act. Your information is shared only with the insurer necessary to issue the policy — never sold, never misused, never handed to any third party. All communication happens over encrypted channels, and you can request deletion of your data any time.

What all insurance categories do you cover?

We cover 15+ categories: Health (Individual, Family Floater, Senior Citizen, Critical Illness, Top-Up), Life (Term, Whole Life, Endowment, ULIP, Child Plans), Motor (Car, Two-Wheeler, Commercial Vehicle), Travel (Domestic & International, Student, Senior), Home, Shopkeeper, Business & Liability, Personal Accident, Group Health, Marine, and more. If it's insurable in India, chances are we cover it.

Which is better — online insurance or through an advisor?

Price-wise, both are usually identical because IRDAI regulates premiums. But an advisor adds real value: they compare 20–30 plans across insurers instead of just one company, flag exclusions you might miss, guide you on the right sum assured, and most importantly, stand by you during claims — the moment that actually matters. With SAS, you get the convenience of online purchase plus the hand-holding of an expert.

At what age should I buy health & life insurance?

As early as possible. Premiums for health and term life insurance are lowest in your 20s and early 30s and rise sharply with age — buying early also means fewer medical tests, lower exclusions and a locked-in rate for decades. A healthy 28-year-old can typically buy a ₹1 crore term cover for less than ₹700/month. Waiting even 5 years can double the cost.
